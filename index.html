Elias Hossain

Hello! I’m Elias, a graduate student at Mississippi State University, USA, where I focus on natural language processing and machine learning. I grew up in Bangladesh, where I also earned my bachelor’s degree. My research journey began in 2017, a time before AI-driven tools and cutting-edge technologies were widely accessible. I initially enjoyed developing mobile applications, primarily using Java, which was the standard before Kotlin became popular. Since then, I’ve participated in various software exhibitions at both national and international levels, receiving several awards. One of my proudest moments was winning the divisional championship in the 2017 NASA Space Apps Challenge, where our team was recognized for the best use of NASA data resources. With approximately three years of industry experience, I've focused primarily on AI applications in healthcare, as well as developing speech-to-text (STT) and text-to-speech (TTS) models for low-resource languages. My time as a software engineer at REVE Systems was especially rewarding—I worked on custom extensions for desktop, web, and Linux applications, making it a challenging and engaging chapter in my professional journey. At Mississippi State, I’ve contributed to projects like BarkPlug, a chatbot powered by large language models designed to provide students with seamless access to engineering department resources. I also worked on an NIH-funded project in collaboration with the Psychology Department during the summer of 2024. For me, research goes beyond generating results or publishing papers. It’s about identifying meaningful problems and solving them in impactful ways. However, I’m excited about what lies ahead and grateful for these opportunities to make a difference in my field.
